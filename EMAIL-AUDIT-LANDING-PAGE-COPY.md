# Digital Movement UK Email Campaign Landing Page

## Goal

Get cold email visitors to submit the audit form so Martey can review their clinic, show the strongest growth opportunities, and move the lead toward a call.

## Page Rules

- One goal only: form submission
- No full website navigation
- Same promise as the email button and subject line
- Form visible high on the page on desktop and mobile
- Keep form to 5 fields or fewer
- Put proof and risk-reversal close to the form
- Keep the copy short, plain, and easy to scan

## Recommended Form Fields

Use these fields:

1. First name
2. Clinic name
3. Website
4. Work email
5. Phone number

If friction is too high, make phone optional first.

Button text:

`Get My Free Audit`

Helper text under the button:

`No pressure. We will show you the best services, areas, and page opportunities first.`

## Recommended Layout Notes

- Hero with copy on the left and form on the right on desktop
- On mobile, stack the copy first and form immediately after
- Remove top navigation links that take people away
- Keep one main CTA style across the whole page
- Repeat the form or CTA after the audit section and again at the bottom
- Put the risk-reversal section above the FAQ, not buried in the footer
- Keep sections tight and card-based so the page is easy to scan

## CRM And Funnel Notes

- Send all form submissions straight to the CRM
- Capture hidden fields for `utm_source`, `utm_medium`, `utm_campaign`, `audience_type`, and `email_variant`
- Route successful submissions to a clear thank-you page
- On the thank-you page, explain what happens next instead of only saying `thank you`

## Best-Practice Notes Used For This Structure

- Message match between email and page
- One clear CTA and minimal distractions
- Value and form high on the page
- Short form with only the fields needed
- Proof close to the ask
- Thank-you page used as the next step, not an afterthought
